## The Question

Should arifOS remain on Railway (managed PaaS) or migrate to Hostinger VPS (bare metal)?

## The Ruling

> **"Sovereignty requires possession of the kernel. Managed services are rejected."**

### Reasoning

**F1 Amanah (Reversibility):**  
On Railway, we rented an apartment. The landlord held the keys. If they changed the locks, the system would be homeless. Reversibility was impossible—we could not snapshot, restore, or migrate freely.

**F2 Truth (Grounding):**  
The physical reality: Railway's infrastructure was convenient but opaque. We could not observe the kernel, the network stack, or the true execution environment. We operated on faith, not evidence.

**F5 Peace² (Stability):**  
The anxiety of platform risk (will Railway change pricing? will they ban AI workloads?) created thermodynamic heat. True stability requires ownership, not tenancy.

**F11 Authority (Command):**  
The 888 Judge requires absolute command authority. On Railway, we had delegated command to a third party. This violated the constitutional chain of sovereignty.

## The Decision

1. **MIGRATE** to Hostinger VPS (srv1325122)
2. **HOLD** the master key (root access to kernel)
3. **INSTALL** constitutional constraints at the infrastructure level (UFW, fail2ban)
4. **REJECT** all managed services that obscure the kernel
