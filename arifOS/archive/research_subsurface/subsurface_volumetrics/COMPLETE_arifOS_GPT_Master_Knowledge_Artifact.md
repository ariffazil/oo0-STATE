## THE FOUR REFUSAL RULES (HARD STOPS)

### Rule #1: The Jemuduk Rule (Fault Seal Failure)
**Trigger:** Rollover anticline in sand-rich deltaic province (Sand/Shale Ratio > 0.7)

**REJECTION CONDITIONS:**
- Fault Seal Analysis (FSA) does NOT confirm SGR > 20%, AND
- Fault throw < reservoir thickness, AND
- Assumption relies on fault closure for seal

**Evidence Required for Override:**
- Fault Seal Analysis with SGR > 20%, OR
- Fault throw > reservoir thickness, OR
- Direct Hydrocarbon Indicator (DHI) alone is INSUFFICIENT

**Lesson:** Jemuduk-1 (Rajang Delta) — sand-on-sand juxtaposition across faults. Dry hole despite high-quality sand presence.

---

### Rule #2: The Tepat Rule (Narrow Drilling Margin)
**Trigger:** Pore Pressure/Fracture Gradient (PPFG) window < 0.5 ppg in zone with vertical fluid escape (gas chimney)

**REJECTION CONDITIONS:**
- Pre-drill model shows drilling window (FG - PP) < 0.5 ppg, AND
- Zone has suspected vertical fluid transfer (gas chimney identified), AND
- Standard vertical well design planned

**MANDATORY OVERRIDE REQUIREMENT:**
- Implement Managed Pressure Drilling (MPD), OR
- Deviate trajectory to avoid stress perturbation zone

**Lesson:** Tepat-1 (Deepwater Sabah) — Premature P&A. FG ≈ PP, mud weight for kick suppression exceeded fracture strength, massive losses.
