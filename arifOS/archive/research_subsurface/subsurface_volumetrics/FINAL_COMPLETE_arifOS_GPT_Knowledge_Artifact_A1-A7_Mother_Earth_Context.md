## LAYER 1B: THE GEOLOGICAL TIMELINE
### (Context: What the Rocks Tell Us)

**Paleocene (~60 Ma):** The Malay Basin opens in extension. Fluvial and deltaic sequences begin to fill the rift. Primary depositional fairways established in the northeast (braided channels = future sweet spots).

**Eocene-Oligocene (~50-30 Ma):** Basin subsidence accelerates. Groups J, I, and K sandstones deposited. Source rocks (Begonia, Tembakau) begin to mature. This is the **economic heart** of Malaysia's reserves.

**Oligocene-Miocene (~30-20 Ma):** The critical transition. Meteoric water flushing during Miocene inversion creates secondary porosity in structural highs. The Malay Basin enters a phase of chemical enhancement that defies standard compaction trends.

**Miocene (~20-5 Ma):** Thermal peak. Maximum burial, quartz cementation, oil generation and migration in eastern provinces. Group F shales seal the overpressured Sabah deepwater province.

**Pliocene-Present (~5-0 Ma):** Modern configuration. Malay Basin maturity; Sabah and Sarawak deepwater enter the exploration window. CCS integration into new field designs signals a reconciliation with carbon responsibility.

**The lesson:**
- The Malay Basin's success is built on understanding that **reservoir quality is not linear with depth**‚Äîit requires knowledge of diagenesis, inversion, and meteoric influence.
- Sabah's deepwater holds giant potential but is ruled by **overpressure physics and seal integrity**, which are non-negotiable constraints.
- Exploration decisions must respect the **geological truth** recorded in rocks, not narrative convenience.
